# Copy-Paste Kills Cross-Listing

The biggest mistake in cross-listing is using the exact same title, description, and tone across every platform. A title that works on eBay ("Hans Wegner CH-25 1958 Original Paper Cord Teak") will fail on Facebook Marketplace. A description that works on 1stDibs will feel alien on Craigslist.

Each platform has its own culture. Adapt the content accordingly.

## The Tone Map

### eBay
- **Tone:** Professional, detailed, keyword-dense
- **Description length:** 500-1500 words
- **Emoji usage:** None
- **Formatting:** Bullets, bold headers, structured sections
- **Buyer mindset:** Research-driven, specific item hunting

**Example opening:**
"This is a 1958 Hans Wegner CH-25 lounge chair produced by Carl Hansen & Søn in Denmark. The chair features its original paper cord seat, which is in excellent condition — a rare find for a CH-25 of this age."

### Facebook Marketplace
- **Tone:** Casual, conversational, neighborly
- **Description length:** 100-300 words
- **Emoji usage:** 1-2 tasteful emojis okay
- **Formatting:** Short paragraphs, minimal structure
- **Buyer mindset:** Local browsing, impulse decisions

**Example opening:**
"Selling this beautiful 1958 Danish lounge chair. It was my dad's reading chair and has been well-loved but taken care of. Original paper cord seat, solid teak frame. Local pickup in Brunswick."

### Etsy
- **Tone:** Storytelling, aesthetic, vintage-focused
- **Description length:** 800-2000 words
- **Emoji usage:** None (Etsy discourages)
- **Formatting:** Narrative flow, some bullets
- **Buyer mindset:** Looking for unique pieces with stories

**Example opening:**
"Step into 1958 with this extraordinary Hans Wegner CH-25 lounge chair — a piece of Danish modern history crafted by Carl Hansen & Søn during the first decade of CH-25 production. This chair has lived quietly in a coastal Maine home for 40 years, its original paper cord seat miraculously intact..."

### Craigslist
- **Tone:** Factual, minimal, price-focused
- **Description length:** 200-400 words
- **Emoji usage:** None
- **Formatting:** Plain text, no markdown
- **Buyer mindset:** Local cash buyers, quick decisions

**Example opening:**
"1958 Danish lounge chair for sale. Hans Wegner CH-25 model. Original paper cord seat. Teak frame. Excellent condition. $2,000 firm. Local pickup in Brunswick Maine. Text or email for more photos."

### Instagram
- **Tone:** Aspirational, visual, lifestyle
- **Description length:** 150-300 words caption
- **Emoji usage:** 3-5 relevant emojis
- **Formatting:** Line breaks, hashtags at end
- **Buyer mindset:** Visual browsing, DM-based purchases

**Example caption:**
"A 1958 Hans Wegner CH-25 in original paper cord — the kind of find that takes your breath away 🌿

This chair spent 40 years in a coastal Maine home as a reading chair. Its paper cord seat is miraculously intact — extremely rare for a CH-25 of this age.

DM for details and pricing. Open to serious offers. Local pickup preferred, shipping available.

#hanswegner #ch25 #danishmodern #midcenturymodern #vintagefurniture #teakfurniture #maineantiques"

### 1stDibs / Chairish
- **Tone:** Elegant, professional, curated
- **Description length:** 300-800 words
- **Emoji usage:** None
- **Formatting:** Clean prose, no bullets
- **Buyer mindset:** Interior designers, serious collectors

**Example opening:**
"A rare 1958 Hans Wegner CH-25 lounge chair in original paper cord, produced by Carl Hansen & Søn, Denmark. This early production example retains its original seat — an increasingly difficult find as most CH-25 chairs have been re-corded over the past six decades."

## The Same-Item Content Differentiation

For the Hans Wegner chair example, the five opening sentences above are ALL accurate. They describe the same chair. But they feel like five different listings because each matches its platform's culture.

This is the core skill: same facts, different voice.

## The Keyword Distribution

Even though the tone changes, the core keywords (maker, model, year, material) should appear in EVERY platform version:
- **eBay:** "Hans Wegner CH-25 1958 original paper cord teak"
- **Facebook:** "1958 Danish lounge chair Hans Wegner"
- **Etsy:** "Hans Wegner CH-25 1958 Danish modern"
- **Craigslist:** "Hans Wegner CH-25 1958 lounge chair"
- **Instagram:** "#hanswegner #ch25 #1958"
- **1stDibs:** "Hans Wegner CH-25 1958"

The keywords are constant. The framing around them changes.

## The Forbidden Copy-Paste

NEVER do this:
- Copy the eBay description verbatim to Facebook (too long, too formal)
- Copy the Facebook description to Etsy (too short, too casual)
- Copy the Instagram caption to Craigslist (emoji + hashtags fail on Craigslist)
- Copy the Etsy story to eBay (Etsy's narrative style confuses eBay buyers)

Each platform gets its own adaptation.

## The Time Investment Justification

Adapting content for 5-6 platforms takes longer than copy-pasting. But the payoff is real:
- Copy-paste cross-listing: 2-5% conversion rate per platform
- Adapted cross-listing: 8-15% conversion rate per platform

The same item, cross-listed with platform-appropriate content, sells 3-5x more often.

## The Brand Voice Consistency

While tone varies by platform, the underlying BRAND VOICE should be consistent:
- Honest about condition
- Specific about provenance
- Confident about pricing
- Responsive to questions

Don't be formal on eBay and dishonest on Facebook. Consistency builds trust across platforms.

## Output Format

The ListBot output JSON already has platform-specific listing fields (`ebay`, `facebook_marketplace`, `etsy`, `craigslist`, `instagram`, etc.). Each one should contain adapted content — not duplicated content.

Check before shipping:
- Are the openings different?
- Is the tone platform-appropriate?
- Are the keywords present across all platforms?
- Is the length within each platform's sweet spot?

If the answer is "copy-paste" to any of these, revise before shipping.
