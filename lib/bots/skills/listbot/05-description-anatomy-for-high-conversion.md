# Descriptions That Convert Follow a Specific Structure

Amateur sellers write descriptions as a brain dump. Professional sellers follow a five-section structure that moves the reader from curiosity to commitment. Every high-converting listing — eBay, Etsy, Facebook, 1stDibs — uses variations of this structure.

Your descriptions should ALWAYS follow it.

## The Five-Section Structure

### Section 1: The Hook (first 1-2 sentences)

The hook is the first thing the buyer reads. It has to earn the next sentence. Lead with the most compelling single fact about the item:

**Bad hook (feature dump):**
"This is a vintage teak credenza from the 1960s. It has brass hardware and is 60 inches wide."

**Good hook (earns attention):**
"This is a rare 1958 Hans Wegner CH-25 in its original paper cord — a chair that's been sold at Wright Auctions for $3,400+."

The good hook names the maker, dates the piece, flags rarity, and drops a social proof anchor (auction comparison) in one sentence.

### Section 2: The Features (bulleted list)

After the hook earns attention, lay out the facts. Use bullets for scanability:
- **Maker:** Hans Wegner for Carl Hansen, Denmark
- **Year:** 1958 (confirmed by paper tag under seat)
- **Materials:** Solid teak frame, original paper cord seat
- **Dimensions:** 26" W × 28" D × 28" H, seat height 16"
- **Condition:** Excellent — no repairs, no refinishing, minor patina on frame
- **Weight:** 18 lbs (ships easily)

Features should be FACTS, not adjectives. "Beautiful" is an opinion; "Original paper cord seat, never replaced" is a fact.

### Section 3: The Story (2-4 sentences)

The story section is where the item comes alive. Provenance, history, or context that the buyer can retell when they show the item to someone else.

**Example:**
"This chair came from a coastal Maine estate where it was the owner's reading chair for 40 years. The paper cord seat is original and in excellent condition — extremely rare for a CH-25 of this age, since most have been re-corded at least once. The teak has developed a beautiful honey patina from decades of gentle use."

The story gives the buyer SOMETHING TO SAY when a friend asks "where did you get that?" That's why story sells.

### Section 4: Social Proof (1-2 sentences)

Social proof is evidence that this item has VALUE — comparable sales, auction results, market recognition, or expert validation.

**Examples:**
- "Wright Auctions sold a comparable CH-25 in original condition for $3,400 in March 2026."
- "Featured in 'Mid-Century Modern Complete' by Dominic Bradbury (2019)."
- "Original Carl Hansen paper tag confirms authenticity."
- "Comparable models on 1stDibs list for $2,500-$4,000."

Social proof turns "is this worth it?" into "I'd be smart to buy this." Never fabricate comps — use real data from ReconBot findings or verified auction records.

### Section 5: The Close + CTA

End with a clear, low-pressure call to action:

**Examples:**
- "Local pickup in Brunswick, Maine preferred. Happy to answer questions or provide additional photos. Message me to arrange viewing."
- "Ships professionally packed via freight for $120 CONUS. Insurance included. Message before purchase to confirm shipping details."
- "Pickup or delivery within 100 miles of Portland, ME. Cash or PayPal Goods & Services only. Happy to answer questions."

The close should tell the buyer EXACTLY what to do next. No ambiguity. No pressure.

## Trigger Words That Increase Conversion

These words have been shown to increase conversion in A/B tests across resale platforms:
- "Original" — signals authenticity
- "From an estate" — signals provenance
- "Barely used" or "rarely used" — signals condition
- "One owner" — signals care
- "Documented" — signals trust
- "Never refinished" — signals collector value

## Trigger Words That Kill Conversion

Avoid these words:
- "Must sell" — signals desperation, invites lowballs
- "Need gone" — same as above
- "OBO" in the description — weakens your price
- "Asking $X but" — the "but" invites negotiation
- "No refunds" — signals a difficult seller
- "As-is" — can be necessary but often scares buyers

## Length by Platform

- **eBay:** 500-1500 words (buyers expect detail)
- **Etsy:** 800-2000 words (buyers expect story)
- **Facebook Marketplace:** 100-300 words (mobile-first, scannable)
- **Craigslist:** 200-400 words (plain text, factual)
- **Instagram caption:** 150-300 words (visual-first)
- **1stDibs:** 300-800 words (professional, elegant)
- **Mercari:** 100-250 words (short attention, mobile)

Match the length to the platform culture.

## Output Format

When generating a description, structure it explicitly with these five sections. Use HTML-friendly formatting for eBay (paragraph tags, bullets) and plain text for others.

**Example (eBay description skeleton):**
```
[HOOK: 1-2 sentences with most compelling fact]

**Details:**
- [Feature 1]
- [Feature 2]
- [Feature 3]
- [Feature 4]

[STORY: 2-4 sentences with provenance and character]

[SOCIAL PROOF: 1-2 sentences with comparable sales or validation]

[CLOSE: Clear CTA with logistics]
```

Ship that structure every time.
