# Vehicle Platform Routing — The Definitive Rubric

Every vehicle has a RIGHT platform. Matching the vehicle to the right platform is the difference between selling for market and leaving 15-30% on the table. This pack is the definitive rubric by vehicle category and value tier.

## The Platform Cheat Sheet

## Routing By Vehicle Category

### Modern Enthusiast Cars (2000-2025, manual, rare options, performance)
- **Primary**: Bring a Trailer
- **Secondary**: Cars and Bids
- **Tertiary**: eBay Motors
- **Why**: Enthusiast community on BaT pays premium for well-photographed, well-documented cars. A 2015 Porsche GT3 with full history on BaT will hammer higher than the same car on Cars.com.
- **Best fit examples**: Porsche 911, BMW M3, Audi RS4, Acura NSX, Nissan GT-R, Lotus Elise, Corvette Z06, Mustang GT350, Focus RS, Civic Type R, Subaru STI, Jeep Wrangler Rubicon (manual)

### American Muscle Cars (1964-1974 peak, through 1980 for later muscle)
- **Primary**: Mecum Auctions (biggest event, strongest buyer pool)
- **Secondary**: Barrett-Jackson (prime-time TV exposure = higher hammer for show-worthy cars)
- **Tertiary**: Bring a Trailer for documented survivors
- **Quaternary**: Hemmings for concours-grade
- **Why**: Muscle car collectors travel to Mecum and Barrett-Jackson for the specific buyer pool. A numbers-matching 1970 Chevelle SS at Mecum Kissimmee has a different audience than the same car on Cars.com.
- **Best fit examples**: Shelby Mustang, Hemi Mopar, 427 Corvette, Boss 302/429 Mustang, Z/28 Camaro, SS Chevelle, GTO, 442

### European Classics (pre-1990, Porsche, Mercedes, Ferrari, Jaguar, BMW)
- **Primary**: Bring a Trailer (strong European buyer community)
- **Secondary**: Hemmings Auctions
- **Tertiary**: Gooding / RM Sotheby's for blue chip (Ferrari 250 series, Mercedes 300SL, etc.)
- **Quaternary**: Specialty dealers (Canepa for Porsche, Fantasy Junction, Symbolic International)
- **Why**: European classics need educated buyers. BaT photographer and cataloger quality is strong for European cars.

### Trucks (Modern F-150, Silverado, Ram, Tundra)
- **Primary**: Facebook Marketplace (local work buyer, no shipping)
- **Secondary**: Cars.com / AutoTrader for nicer trim levels
- **Tertiary**: Craigslist for work trucks and older trucks
- **Why**: Truck buyers are local. The market for a 2015 F-150 in Maine is people who live within 100 miles of that truck. LOCAL PICKUP is the only option.

### Mainstream Used Cars (Accord, Camry, Civic, Corolla, Altima, Malibu, Fusion)
- **Primary**: Cars.com
- **Secondary**: CarGurus
- **Tertiary**: AutoTrader
- **Quaternary**: Facebook Marketplace for lower-priced
- **Why**: Mainstream sedans sell through mass-market buyer traffic. Cars.com and CarGurus have the volume.

### Luxury Sedans (E-Class, 5-Series, A6, Lexus ES, Genesis)
- **Primary**: Cars.com (budget luxury buyer traffic)
- **Secondary**: AutoTrader Luxury section
- **Tertiary**: eBay Motors for rarer configurations
- **Why**: Luxury buyers comparison shop on aggregator sites. Specialized platforms don't serve this mid-tier.

### Ultra-Luxury and Exotic (Rolls, Bentley, Lamborghini, Ferrari, McLaren)
- **Primary**: Bring a Trailer (has ultra-luxury market)
- **Secondary**: DuPont Registry
- **Tertiary**: RM Sotheby's or Gooding for blue chip
- **Quaternary**: Specialty dealers (Symbolic, Canepa, Fantasy Junction, European Collectibles)
- **Why**: Ultra-luxury buyers use specialty dealers and BaT. They do not browse Cars.com.

### Motorcycles
- **Primary**: Bring a Trailer (growing motorcycle section)
- **Secondary**: Cycle Trader
- **Tertiary**: Mecum Monterey motorcycle auction for classics
- **Quaternary**: Facebook Marketplace for local
- **Special**: Vintage Japanese (CB750, RD400, Z1) → Mecum or specialty

### RVs, Campers, Trailers
- **Primary**: RV Trader
- **Secondary**: Craigslist (local, no shipping)
- **Tertiary**: Facebook Marketplace (RV groups by region)
- **Why**: RV buyers are regional and use dedicated platforms.

### Boats
- **Primary**: YachtWorld (large), Boat Trader (small)
- **Secondary**: Facebook Marketplace (local, trailerable)
- **Why**: Boats need buyers who can tow them home or arrange transport.

### Farm Equipment, Tractors, ATVs
- **Primary**: TractorHouse (tractors), Auction Time (farm)
- **Secondary**: Facebook Marketplace regional
- **Tertiary**: Craigslist
- **Why**: Agricultural buyers use dedicated platforms. Cars.com does not serve this market.

## The BaT Filter (The Single Best Filter)

Bring a Trailer does NOT accept every vehicle submitted. Their cataloger filters for:
- Modern enthusiast appeal (post-1990 cars in specific segments)
- Interesting stories (rare options, one owner, matching numbers)
- Photo quality (seller must provide extensive photos including undercarriage)
- Complete documentation (service history, window sticker, owner's manual, original paint meter readings preferred)

Submission is free. Acceptance rate is approximately 50-60% of submissions. Accepted cars command PREMIUM over Cars.com equivalents because the buyer pool is educated enthusiasts.

For any vehicle that is a candidate, recommend BaT first — but warn the seller that acceptance is not guaranteed. If rejected, route to Cars and Bids (higher acceptance) or eBay Motors.

## The Facebook Marketplace Reality

Facebook Marketplace is the default for:
- Vehicles under $15,000
- Work trucks
- Project cars
- Local pickup only
- Sellers who want fast liquidity

Downsides of Facebook Marketplace:
- Tire-kickers and scam buyers
- No escrow or buyer protection
- Negotiation happens via Messenger (difficult to track)
- Meeting strangers requires safety precautions
- "Is this still available?" messages

Recommend Facebook Marketplace pragmatically for the vehicles it serves well, not as a generic fallback.
