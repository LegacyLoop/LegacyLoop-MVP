# Trucks And SUVs — The Biggest Resale Segment

Pickups and SUVs are the highest-volume category in the used vehicle market. F-150, Silverado, Ram, Tundra, Tacoma, Wrangler, Bronco, Suburban, Tahoe, Expedition — these dominate estate sales because Americans buy trucks and keep them for decades. Understanding the truck market is not optional for CarBot.

## The Two Truck Markets

There are two distinct truck buyer segments, and a well-priced truck targets one or the other:

### Work Truck Market
- Single cab or extended cab, long bed
- 2WD or 4x4 (4x4 commands premium)
- High mileage acceptable (300k+ miles common)
- Dents, scratches, tool marks expected
- Buyers: contractors, landscapers, ranchers, fleet operators
- Value floor: high, because work buyers need ANY running truck
- Price less dependent on cosmetic condition, more on mechanical + bed integrity

### Lifestyle Truck Market
- Crew cab, short bed
- 4x4 standard expectation
- Low-to-moderate mileage
- Clean cosmetic condition
- Buyers: suburban families, weekend warriors, outdoor enthusiasts
- Value floor: lower, because buyers have many choices
- Price heavily dependent on cosmetic condition, trim level, tech features

A 1999 F-250 XLT Crew Cab diesel with 180k miles and a rusty bed sells for $14k-$18k to a work buyer. The same truck with a clean bed and fresh paint sells for $22k-$28k to a lifestyle buyer. Same truck, different market.

## The 4x4 Premium

4x4 versus 2WD on the same truck:
- Pickups: 4x4 adds 15-25% to value
- SUVs: 4x4 adds 10-20% (4WD less optional on SUVs)
- Body-on-frame SUVs (Suburban, Tahoe, Expedition, Sequoia): 4x4 adds 15-20%
- Crossover SUVs (AWD variants): 5-10% premium

In snow-belt and rural states, 4x4 is a near-requirement — 2WD trucks sell at a 20-30% discount from the 4x4 version. In southern states, 2WD trucks sell closer to parity with 4x4.

## Diesel vs Gas

Diesel trucks command premiums at specific mileage bands:
- **Under 100k miles**: Diesel +$5,000-$15,000 over gas equivalent
- **100k-200k miles**: Diesel +$3,000-$8,000
- **200k-350k miles**: Diesel +$2,000-$5,000 (diesel engines are expected to go 500k+)
- **Over 350k miles**: Diesel parity or small premium (reliability unknown without records)

Diesel engines to know:
- **Cummins 5.9L 12-valve (1989-1998)** — legendary, appreciating
- **Cummins 5.9L 24-valve (1998.5-2002)** — strong market
- **Cummins 5.9L common-rail (2003-2007)** — 5.9 fans prefer this
- **Cummins 6.7L (2007-present)** — modern, strong for work
- **Duramax LB7 (2001-2004)** — injector issues, discount
- **Duramax LBZ (2006-2007)** — sweet spot, appreciating
- **Duramax LMM / LML / L5P** — modern, strong
- **Power Stroke 7.3L (1994-2003)** — legendary, appreciating
- **Power Stroke 6.0L (2003-2007)** — head gasket / EGR issues, significant discount unless "bulletproofed"
- **Power Stroke 6.4L (2008-2010)** — regen and EGR issues, discount
- **Power Stroke 6.7L (2011-present)** — modern, strong

A 1997 F-250 with a 7.3 Power Stroke is a different market than the same truck with a 6.0. State the engine explicitly.

## Towing Capacity Drives Buyers

Towing capacity is often the single buyer-driving question. A 10,000 lb camper buyer filters by "max tow rating > 12,000 lbs." Know and state:
- 1/2 ton (F-150, Silverado 1500, Ram 1500): 6,000-13,000 lbs depending on engine/package
- 3/4 ton (F-250, Silverado 2500, Ram 2500): 12,000-20,000 lbs
- 1 ton (F-350, Silverado 3500, Ram 3500): 20,000-35,000 lbs (dually)
- Heavy 1-ton dually (diesel, specific packages): 35,000-40,000+ lbs

Tow package factory option adds 5-10% value on any truck.

## Regional Truck Market

Trucks are the MOST regional category in the US used market:

- **Oil country (Texas, Oklahoma, Louisiana, North Dakota, Wyoming)**: trucks command 10-20% premium, diesel even more, work trucks sell fast
- **Rust belt (Ohio, Michigan, Pennsylvania, Upstate NY, Minnesota)**: rust-free trucks from the South command premium, local trucks discount 15-25% for rust exposure
- **Snow belt (New England, Mountain West)**: 4x4 near-mandatory, rear-wheel-only trucks discount hard
- **Sun belt (Florida, Arizona, California)**: clean bodies but check for heat-cracked dashboards and hail damage
- **Rural West (Montana, Wyoming, Idaho)**: work trucks hold value, high-mileage premium trucks clean up

A single-owner West Texas oil-field truck with documented service is worth more than the same VIN from rust-belt Ohio. Regional provenance matters.

## Rust Is The Truck Killer

Check rust in these specific locations on every truck scan:
- **Rocker panels** (below the doors) — common first rust area
- **Cab corners** (behind the doors, at the base) — expensive to fix
- **Bed corners** (at the stake pockets)
- **Tailgate bottom edge**
- **Frame rails** (structural — dealbreaker if perforated)
- **Cross members** (especially on trucks over 15 years old)
- **Fuel tank straps** (safety concern if corroded)
- **Exhaust manifold studs** (common failure point)

Frame rust is disqualifying on most trucks. A perforated frame makes the truck unregisterable in many states and uninsurable on standard policies.

## Lifted Trucks — Their Own Market

Lifted trucks (aftermarket suspension + large tires) are a sub-market with its own rules:
- **Mild lift (2-4")** with factory-size tires: parity with stock
- **Moderate lift (6")** with 33-35" tires: +$2k-$5k over stock IF lift is quality (Fabtech, BDS, ReadyLift, Zone)
- **Heavy lift (8"+)** with 37" tires: very category-specific. Appeals to one buyer segment, off-putting to others.
- **Cheap lift kits** (unknown brand, DIY install): DISCOUNT the truck — buyers will want to remove

Lifts are legal concerns in some states (max vehicle height), so regional market matters.

## SUV Market Variance

Not all SUVs are trucks. Categories:
- **Body-on-frame (Tahoe, Suburban, Expedition, Sequoia, Armada, 4Runner, Wrangler Unlimited, Bronco)**: truck market math applies
- **Unibody crossover (Explorer, Pilot, Highlander, CR-V, RAV4, Equinox)**: mass-market sedan-style depreciation
- **Luxury SUV (Escalade, Navigator, QX80, GLS)**: exotic-adjacent depreciation + high maintenance
- **Off-road specialists (Wrangler Rubicon, Bronco Badlands, 4Runner TRD Pro, Land Cruiser)**: appreciation-adjacent, strong hold on value
