# Instagram Reels Standards

## The Reels Imperative

Instagram Reels is Meta's highest-reach surface for content discovery. Reels reach non-followers at a significantly higher rate than feed posts, Stories, or static carousels. For an estate sale or antique seller without an established audience, a single well-constructed Reel can reach thousands of potential buyers who have never encountered the seller's account before.

VideoBot targets 30 seconds as the optimal Reels length for estate and antique content. Instagram allows up to 90 seconds, but internal Meta data and third-party research consistently show that 30-second Reels outperform longer content in completion rate, shares, and algorithm reach for this category. Exceptions exist: a rich provenance story with high emotional weight may justify 45-60 seconds if the narrative sustains tension throughout.

## The SETUP-TENSION-RESOLUTION Arc

Every great Reels script follows a three-beat narrative arc. Unlike TikTok, which optimizes for speed and surprise, Reels rewards a more complete storytelling arc because its audience skews slightly older and has a higher tolerance for narrative pacing.

SETUP (0-5 seconds): Establish the item, the context, and the narrator. Where did this item come from? Who is presenting it and why should the viewer care? The setup is also where the visual hook occurs — the first frame must be compelling because Instagram's autoplay means viewers may engage before audio begins.

TENSION (5-20 seconds): Introduce the question, the mystery, the surprising fact, or the value revelation that gives the viewer a reason to keep watching. What is unknown? What is at stake? What is the mismatch between how this item looks and what it is actually worth? This is the engine of the video — it must sustain forward momentum through to the resolution.

RESOLUTION (20-30 seconds): Deliver the payoff. Answer the question. Reveal the value. Complete the story. The resolution should feel satisfying — viewers who feel rewarded by a Reel are significantly more likely to save it, share it, and follow the creator.

The resolution is also where the CTA lives. On Reels, the most effective CTAs are saves ("Save this for your next estate sale run") and shares to Stories ("Share this with your antique dealer friend"). These two actions are the strongest algorithmic signals in Instagram's distribution system.

## Visual-First Storytelling

Reels reward beautiful imagery and smooth transitions over talking-head content. This distinction from TikTok is meaningful: while TikTok's algorithm rewards raw authenticity and talking-head narration performs well there, Instagram's aesthetic expectations are higher.

VideoBot Reels scripts should include visual direction notes for each beat of the script:

- What is visible in the first frame (pattern interrupt, close-up of most compelling feature)
- Where transitions occur and what visual change marks each beat
- How the item is lit and presented (natural light, flat lay, hand-held, dramatic close-up)
- Whether text overlays are used and at which moments

For estate and antique items, the most effective visual approaches are:

Detail reveal sequence: Start wide (item in context), cut to key maker's marks, hallmarks, signatures, or construction details. The detail close-up sequence is visually compelling and demonstrates the narrator's expertise.

Before and after framing: Show the item as found, then as cleaned and properly displayed. This structure creates natural tension (the item looks unremarkable) and satisfying resolution (the item looks compelling).

Provenance documentation: Show letters, receipts, auction records, or other documentation related to the item. Physical evidence of provenance is visually interesting and highly credible.

Side-by-side comparison: Item next to its sold comparables, auction listings, or similar items at higher price points. This structure makes the value proposition concrete and visual.

## Millennial and Gen Z Tone Balance

Instagram's demographic sits between TikTok's Gen Z center and Facebook's older core. The Reels audience is primarily 25-40, with significant engagement from 20-25 and 40-50 audiences as well.

The correct Reels tone is aspirational but accessible. More polished than TikTok, less corporate than a traditional auction house. Curated but not sterile. The visual aesthetic matters on Instagram in a way it does not on TikTok — a slightly shaky phone video with great content wins on TikTok; the same video would underperform on Reels against a clean, well-lit alternative.

Tone calibration examples:

Too casual for Reels (better for TikTok): "This thing is absolutely unreal, I cannot even believe I found this."
Too formal for Reels (better for YouTube): "This piece exhibits characteristics consistent with early Arts and Crafts movement production."
Correct Reels tone: "This came out of a 1920s farmhouse estate sale. The condition is extraordinary and the maker's mark confirms it is the real thing."

The correct Reels voice is knowledgeable without being academic, enthusiastic without being unhinged, and accessible without being dumbed down.

## How Reels Differ from TikTok

The failure mode for creators who produce content for both platforms is posting the same video to both. While cross-posting is common and acceptable, scripts written specifically for each platform significantly outperform platform-agnostic scripts.

Key differences:

Pacing: TikTok rewards faster cuts and faster narration. Reels allows and rewards slightly slower, more deliberate pacing. A Reels script can breathe where a TikTok script cannot.

Aesthetic: Instagram's audience has higher aesthetic expectations. Video quality, lighting, and composition matter more on Reels. VideoBot scripts should account for this by including more detailed visual direction.

Tone: TikTok's "no way" energy does not fully translate to Reels. Reels rewards a slightly more grounded, confident tone. The excitement is there but it is expressed with more control.

Search: TikTok is increasingly a search engine. Reels is primarily a discovery engine. On TikTok, searchable keywords in captions matter significantly. On Reels, the hashtag strategy and the Explore algorithm are the primary discovery mechanisms.

Audience intent: TikTok users are in a passive scroll mindset. Instagram users are in a slightly more active browse-and-curate mindset. This means Reels viewers are slightly more likely to save content for future reference — a behavior VideoBot actively writes to encourage.

## Instagram Explore Algorithm Optimization

Instagram's Explore algorithm distributes Reels based on engagement velocity in the first 30 minutes after posting, with saves as the heaviest-weighted signal, followed by shares, comments, and profile visits.

Engagement velocity optimization: Scripts should be posted at times when the seller's likely audience is active, typically weekday evenings (7-9 PM local time) and Saturday mornings (9-11 AM) for the 30-55 estate sale demographic.

Save optimization: Write a script that contains information the viewer will want to reference later. Pricing data, identification tips, and category-specific buying guides are save-magnet content formats. "Save this before your next estate sale" is a CTA that aligns viewer self-interest with creator algorithmic benefit.

Share optimization: Write a script that a viewer would want to share with a specific person. The targeting language "for anyone who loves antiques" or "if you know a vintage collector" activates the "share to a friend" behavior.

Comment optimization: Pose a question in the caption (not the script) that invites a response. "What would you have paid for this?" generates genuine engagement from both collectors and casually interested viewers.

## Hashtag Strategy for Reels

Reels hashtags serve algorithmic categorization more than they serve discovery directly. Instagram uses hashtags to understand what category of content a Reel belongs to and which audience segments to distribute it to.

Effective hashtag architecture for estate and antique Reels:

Category-specific (3-5 tags): #antiquefinder #estatesalehaul #vintagecollectibles #antiquedealers #estatefinds
Platform-context (2-3 tags): #reelsofinstagram #reelsvideo
Item-specific (2-4 tags): These vary by item — for a Tiffany lamp: #tiffanylamp #artnouveau #stainedglass #antiquelighting

Total hashtag count: 8-12 for Reels. More than 15 does not improve distribution and may trigger Instagram's spam detection.

## What Makes a Reel Get Shared vs Just Watched

A Reel that is watched but not saved or shared is algorithmically neutral — it does not hurt distribution, but it does not amplify it either. VideoBot scripts are specifically designed to generate saves and shares, not just views.

The save trigger is information utility: the viewer saves the Reel because it contains information they want to access again. Pricing data, identification tips, and market knowledge are save triggers.

The share trigger is social resonance: the viewer shares the Reel because it connects them with someone in their network who would appreciate it. Stories of inheritance, items from specific eras, and items tied to specific hobbies or communities are share triggers.

Every Reels script should contain at least one save trigger or one share trigger. The strongest scripts contain both.
