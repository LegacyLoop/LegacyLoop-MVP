# Call to Action Mastery

## Why the CTA Is the Most Consequential Element in the Script

A video without a CTA is a performance, not a sales tool. The CTA converts passive viewing into active engagement. It is the moment when the viewer decides whether to keep scrolling or to do something.

Most video scripts put the most effort into the hook and the body. The CTA is treated as an afterthought — a boilerplate sentence appended to the end. This is backwards.

The CTA deserves the same level of craft as the hook. It should be specific, platform-native, and designed to create the exact behavior that converts most reliably for that platform and that item category.

VideoBot generates a dedicated CTA for every script. This pack defines the rules.

## The CTA Hierarchy by Conversion Strength

Not all CTAs convert equally. Ranked by their ability to generate purchase-intent engagement:

Rank 1: "DM me [keyword] for price"

This is the highest-converting CTA format on short-form video platforms. It works because it requires the viewer to take an active step — sending a direct message — which creates a direct conversation between seller and buyer. Once that conversation starts, the seller has the full attention of a qualified lead. The keyword requirement (DM me CHAIR, DM me TABLE, DM me VIN) also allows the seller to track which video drove which inquiry, and gives the buyer a clear, low-friction action to take.

Why the keyword works: "DM me for price" is vague. The viewer has to think about what to say. "DM me CHAIR for dimensions and price" tells the viewer exactly what to type. Removing that decision friction measurably increases DM rate.

Rank 2: "Comment WANT below"

Comment-driving CTAs produce two benefits simultaneously: they generate algorithm engagement signals (comments are weighted more heavily than likes on most platforms), and they create a visible, public list of interested buyers. When a viewer sees 47 comments saying WANT, they feel urgency — this item has competition. Comment CTAs work best for lower-priced items where the barrier to expression is low.

Rank 3: "Link in bio"

This is a standard CTA that redirects viewers to a listing page, storefront, or purchase link. It is passive — the viewer must take two steps (go to profile, click link) instead of one. It works consistently but converts at a lower rate than direct DM or comment CTAs because each additional step loses some percentage of interested viewers.

Use "link in bio" for: higher-priced items where a full listing page provides necessary context, items with multiple photos that cannot be conveyed in a short video, or items where the purchase process requires more than a quick DM.

Rank 4: "Follow for more"

This is a growth CTA, not a conversion CTA. It builds an audience over time but drives zero purchase intent in the moment. Include it only when paired with a primary conversion CTA, never as the sole CTA.

Correct: "DM me TABLE for details — and follow for more estate finds this week."
Incorrect: "Follow for more!" (end of video, no purchase CTA)

## Platform-Specific CTA Formats

Each platform has a native CTA format that performs best with its audience.

TikTok: TikTok rewards comment-driving CTAs. The algorithm interprets high comment volume as a signal of engaging content and boosts distribution. The DM keyword CTA also performs well on TikTok because TikTok DMs are a natural part of the platform's commerce behavior. Avoid "link in bio" as the primary TikTok CTA — TikTok users are less likely to leave the app than users on other platforms.

Best TikTok CTA formats:
- "Comment WANT and I will DM you the price."
- "DM me [KEYWORD] for full details."
- "Comment your offer below — first reasonable offer wins."

Instagram: Instagram rewards save and share CTAs because saves and shares signal high-value content to the algorithm. However, for selling purposes, the DM keyword CTA is still the highest-converting format. Instagram users are accustomed to DM commerce through Marketplace and creator accounts.

Best Instagram CTA formats:
- "DM me [KEYWORD] to claim this before it's gone."
- "Save this and DM me when you're ready — I'll hold it 24 hours for serious buyers."
- "Link in bio goes to the full listing with all photos and dimensions."

YouTube Shorts: YouTube rewards subscribe CTAs because subscriber growth is the primary metric YouTube's algorithm uses to determine creator value. However, subscribe CTAs do not convert to sales. For resale content on YouTube Shorts, the hybrid CTA — subscribe for more finds, then a redirect to purchase — works best.

Best YouTube Shorts CTA formats:
- "Subscribe for more estate finds — link in bio for this one while it is still available."
- "Comment below if you want details — I respond to every comment."
- "Check the description for the full listing link."

Facebook Video: Facebook users in buying mode respond to share-to-group CTAs. "Share this to your local estate sale group" or "Tag someone who collects these" activates the Facebook social graph in a way that other platforms cannot replicate. Facebook users are also more comfortable with direct "PM me" language than "DM me."

Best Facebook CTA formats:
- "PM me for details and your local pickup options."
- "Share this with someone who would want it — happy to hold for a referral."
- "Comment INTERESTED and I will send you the full details."

## Urgency Without Desperation

Urgency is a legitimate and effective tool. Desperation is a repellent.

The difference: urgency implies that the item is desirable and time-sensitive. Desperation implies that the seller needs to sell and will negotiate downward under pressure. Sophisticated buyers — particularly in the collector and high-value categories — respond to urgency and avoid sellers who signal desperation.

Urgency language that works:
- "First come, first served — I have had two inquiries already."
- "DM me before it is gone — this one will not sit."
- "I am listing this on three platforms this week. Whoever moves first gets it."
- "Priced to move by this weekend — I have another pickup Saturday."
- "Only available locally — no holds without a confirmed pickup time."

Desperation language that repels buyers:
- "Please buy this, I really need to move it."
- "I desperately need to sell this — any offer considered."
- "Will take anything reasonable, I just need it gone."
- "Make me an offer, I'm very motivated."

Note that "very motivated seller" and "all offers considered" are real estate desperation signals that have migrated into resale culture. They signal that the seller does not know what they have and will accept below-market offers. This may accelerate a sale at the wrong price. For high-value items, these phrases actively reduce the buyer's opening offer because they signal that the seller can be leveraged.

The correct urgency frame: "This is priced right. Someone will take it this week. DM me first." This is confident, specific, and time-sensitive without signaling vulnerability.

## How to Close a Video Script

The last 3-5 seconds are the CTA. They are the most action-oriented moment in the video. The energy must match the intent.

Common mistakes in video closings:
- Trailing off: "So yeah, if you want it, I guess just let me know or something..."
- Repeating what was already said: "So anyway, like I said, this is an antique and it is really nice and valuable and rare..."
- Hedging: "I think it might be worth something, maybe message me if you want more info..."
- Whispering: Volume that drops at the end of a video loses viewers at the moment of conversion

The correct close: state the CTA with the same energy level as the hook. Drop the pace slightly so the CTA can be heard and understood. Use a complete sentence. End on a concrete action verb.

Example of a weak close: "So if you want it, you can, like, DM me or whatever."
Example of a strong close: "DM me CHAIR and I will send you dimensions, the backstory, and the price. First message gets first consideration."

The strong close is specific (what to send), informative (what you get in return), and clear about how priority works (first message). It gives the buyer a reason to act immediately.

## What Makes Someone DM vs Scroll Past

The decision to DM happens in approximately two seconds. The viewer evaluates whether the effort of opening a DM is worth what they might get. The CTA must answer that question before they scroll.

Elements that trigger DM behavior:

Specificity: "DM me CHAIR for price and dimensions" tells the buyer exactly what to type and exactly what they will receive. The specificity signals that the seller has the information ready and will respond promptly. "DM me for info" is vague on both sides.

Social proof: "Two inquiries already this morning" creates competition. The viewer who was 60% interested becomes 80% interested when they believe they might miss out.

Clear next step: The CTA must describe the buyer's exact next action. "Send me a DM" is clear. "Reach out if interested" is not a specific action.

Price presence or absence: Some categories benefit from price disclosure in the video (daily driver vehicles, lower-value items). Other categories benefit from price as the reward for DM engagement (high-value antiques, collector items). The DM-for-price format works when the item is priced above casual impulse purchase threshold and the seller wants to qualify buyers before disclosing.

## Category-Specific CTA Formulas

Furniture and large antiques (TikTok/Instagram):
"DM me [item word] for dimensions, delivery options, and price. Local pickup [city] — I can arrange delivery within [radius] miles for large pieces."

Sports cards and collectibles (TikTok):
"Comment WANT below — I am reading every comment. DM me [item keyword] for grade documentation and asking price."

Vehicles (all platforms):
"Local pickup only. DM me VIN for the full Carfax, service records, and asking price. Serious buyers only — test drives by appointment."

Jewelry and small valuables (Instagram):
"DM me [item keyword] for more photos, measurements, and hallmark documentation. I respond to every inquiry."

Coins and currency (TikTok/YouTube):
"Comment GRADE if you want to discuss the coin. DM me [item keyword] for the certification number so you can pull the population report yourself."

Estate lots and multi-item sells (Facebook):
"PM me for the full inventory list. I have more pieces from this estate that are not in the video. First buyer to come look gets first selection."

Books and ephemera (Instagram/Facebook):
"DM me [item keyword] for condition details, edition information, and price. I ship with insurance on anything over fifty dollars."

Pottery and ceramics (TikTok/Instagram):
"DM me [item keyword] for the maker's mark close-up photos, dimensions, and asking price. This ships safely — I have packed and shipped dozens of pieces without a scratch."
