# Hashtag Strategy

## Why Hashtag Strategy Differs by Platform

Hashtags are a discovery mechanism, not a decoration. Each platform uses them differently, weights them differently in its ranking algorithm, and attracts a different audience through them. A hashtag set that performs on Instagram will underperform on TikTok. A TikTok hashtag strategy applied to YouTube achieves nothing.

VideoBot generates platform-specific hashtag sets for every script output. This pack defines the rules behind those sets.

## TikTok Hashtag Algorithm Rules

TikTok's algorithm uses hashtags as topic signals, not as primary discovery drivers. The For You Page algorithm relies more heavily on watch time, completion rate, and interaction signals than on hashtag matching. However, hashtags still influence early distribution — TikTok uses them to determine the initial audience pool for a new video.

Rule: Use 3-5 hashtags, not 30.

TikTok penalizes hashtag stuffing. A video with 25 hashtags does not reach 5 times the audience of a video with 5 hashtags. It reaches a smaller audience because the algorithm cannot confidently categorize the content. Choose the tags that most specifically describe the item and the transaction intent.

Rule: Mix trending with niche.

One trending tag (high volume, high competition — 500M+ views) alongside two or three niche tags (under 50M views, high relevance) creates the optimal reach-to-relevance ratio. The trending tag gets the video into a large pool. The niche tags get it to the specific audience that will engage most deeply.

Rule: Include a transaction-intent tag.

Tags like #ForSale, #ShopNow, #BuyNow, and #EstateSale signal purchase intent. TikTok Shop has trained the algorithm to recognize transaction-intent content. Including one transaction tag increases the likelihood of reaching viewers who are actively in buying mode.

Evergreen TikTok category tags for resale content:
- #EstateFind — 800M+ views, broad discovery
- #VintageFind — 1.2B+ views, vintage and antique audience
- #ThriftFlip — 600M+ views, resale and arbitrage audience
- #AntiqueTikTok — 200M+ views, collector audience
- #EstateSaleTok — 50M+ views, estate sale specific
- #WhatIsThis — 400M+ views, identification content
- #GarageFind — 150M+ views, discovery content

## Instagram Hashtag Optimization

Instagram allows up to 30 hashtags per post. The optimal range is 8-15. Posts with more than 20 hashtags on Instagram tend to look like spam to human viewers even when they perform adequately in algorithmic reach.

Rule: Size tier mixing.

Mega tags (1M+ posts): reach massive audiences but competition is extreme. Your content is immediately buried unless engagement rate is very high. Include 1-2.

Mid-tier tags (100K-1M posts): the most reliable discovery range for small to mid-size accounts. Include 4-6.

Niche tags (under 100K posts): your content stays visible longer. High-relevance audience. Include 3-5.

Rule: First comment placement.

For Instagram, placing hashtags in the first comment rather than in the caption keeps the caption clean and professional. This matters for high-value items where the caption should read as authoritative, not as a tag dump. The algorithm treats first-comment hashtags identically to caption hashtags.

Rule: Item-specific hashtags outperform category hashtags.

#StickleyFurniture outperforms #AntiqueFurniture for reaching actual Stickley buyers. #PSA10 outperforms #SportsCards for reaching high-grade card collectors. The more specific the hashtag, the more qualified the viewer.

## YouTube Tag Strategy

YouTube Shorts tags are less important than title keywords. The YouTube search and discovery algorithm prioritizes the video title, thumbnail, and watch metrics over tags. However, tags remain relevant for topic categorization on standard YouTube uploads.

Rule: Three to five relevant tags in the description, not the title.

Tags in the YouTube description provide supplementary topic signals. They should match the item category, the transaction intent, and any collector-community terms that YouTube users would search.

Rule: Include #Shorts as the first tag on all short-form content.

This is mandatory for Shorts classification. Without the #Shorts tag, YouTube may not classify the video as a Short and will not distribute it through the Shorts feed.

Rule: Title keywords matter more than tags.

A YouTube Shorts title of "1967 Mustang Fastback — Matching Numbers For Sale" will outperform the same video with a weaker title regardless of tag set quality. Tags should reinforce the title, not compensate for a weak one.

## Facebook Group Hashtag Targeting

Facebook hashtags function differently from other platforms. The Facebook algorithm does not use hashtags as a primary discovery mechanism the way TikTok and Instagram do. Within groups, hashtags matter less than the group-specific language and terminology that the community uses.

Rule: Use 2-3 hashtags maximum on Facebook video posts.

More than this reads as spam to Facebook users, who have been conditioned to see hashtag-heavy posts as low-quality.

Rule: Group-specific language matters more than hashtags.

In a Facebook group called "Estate Sale Pickers of New England," the words "estate sale," "picker," "New England," and community-specific terminology will drive engagement faster than any hashtag. Use the vocabulary of the community.

Rule: Post in the right group before optimizing hashtags.

Facebook group targeting is a placement decision, not a hashtag decision. The highest-value action on Facebook is finding the specific group where buyers of that item congregate, then posting content that uses the community's language. Hashtags are secondary.

Recommended hashtags for Facebook resale content:
- #EstateSale
- #VintageForsale
- #AntiqueFind
- #ClassicCar (vehicle content)
- #CollectorCommunity

## How Hashtags Compound Over Time

Consistent use of the same niche hashtags builds a content account's association with that community over time. This is the compounding effect of hashtag consistency.

An account that uses #EstateSaleTok on every video builds algorithmic association with that tag. After 30-50 videos, TikTok's algorithm begins recommending the account's content to users who have engaged with #EstateSaleTok content before, even before the algorithm has evaluated the new video's performance. This is the hashtag network effect.

The implication: VideoBot should recommend a consistent core set of 2-3 "brand hashtags" for each seller based on their primary item category, and apply them to every video in addition to the item-specific tags.
