# Platform Photo Requirements

## Why Platform-Specific Standards Matter

Each resale platform has a different audience, a different algorithm, and a different visual
language. A photo set optimized for eBay will underperform on Etsy. A lifestyle shot that drives
engagement on Instagram will look out of place in a clinical eBay category listing. PhotoBot must
know the destination platform before it can assess whether a photo set is ready to publish.
Platform-specific compliance is not optional for high-conversion listings — it is the baseline.

## eBay

eBay is a high-volume, search-driven marketplace. Photo standards are tied directly to search
ranking, click-through rate, and buyer trust signals that eBay's algorithm reads.

Minimum requirement: 1 photo. eBay will not create a listing without at least one image.
Practical requirement: 12 photos — eBay allows up to 24 and provides 12 free. Sellers who use
fewer than 8 photos consistently underperform in search results compared to sellers who fill the
full allotment.

Resolution: 1600 pixels minimum on the longest side. eBay's zoom feature requires at least 1600px
for the enhanced view to activate. At lower resolutions, buyers see a blurred zoom overlay, which
reduces purchase confidence for detailed items. 3000px is the recommended target.

Background: White or neutral is strongly preferred. eBay's Cassini search algorithm assigns a
quality score to listings. White or light neutral backgrounds are associated with higher-quality
listings in the algorithm's training data. This is a measurable ranking factor.

First photo rules: The first photo is the hero image and determines the thumbnail displayed in
search results. The thumbnail is the buyer's first impression. It must show the complete item,
full-frame, on a clean background. Cropped or partial hero images reduce click-through rates.

Prohibited: Stock photos from manufacturer catalogs. eBay's image policy prohibits stock photos
for used items and most collectibles. Listings using stock photos risk removal.
Watermarks are discouraged and flagged by eBay's image quality system.
Borders, frames, and overlaid text reduce listing quality scores and are policy violations in
some categories.

## Etsy

Etsy is a curated, lifestyle-driven marketplace. Its buyer base expects visual storytelling and
aesthetic context. The clinical white-background approach that works on eBay actively underperforms
on Etsy.

Photo allowance: Up to 10 photos per listing.
Resolution: 2000x2000 pixels is Etsy's recommended minimum. Square format is the traditional Etsy
standard, though Etsy now supports multiple aspect ratios.

Visual approach: Lifestyle and styled photos outperform white-background photos on Etsy across most
categories. Buyers respond to context — seeing a vintage ceramic bowl on a wooden table beside linen
napkins tells a story that a bowl on white cannot. The exception is small detailed items (jewelry,
stamps, small figurines) where close-up detail shots on neutral backgrounds are necessary to show
craftsmanship.

Video: Etsy listings with a video (up to 15 seconds) receive approximately 40% more engagement than
listings without video. A simple rotating or zooming video of the item on a clean surface is
sufficient. This is one of the highest-ROI additions to any Etsy listing.

First photo: The hero photo must be compelling as a thumbnail, because Etsy's search grid is
visually competitive. The hero must instantly communicate what the item is, what it looks like, and
why it is appealing — in a square thumbnail approximately 170x135 pixels in grid view.

## Facebook Marketplace

Facebook Marketplace is a mobile-first, local-audience platform. Most interactions happen on
phones. Photo optimization must account for mobile viewing conditions.

Photo allowance: Up to 50 photos per listing.
Aspect ratio: Portrait 4:5 or near-square is preferred. Mobile feeds display vertical images larger
than landscape images. A portrait-oriented photo fills more of a phone screen and captures more
visual attention in the feed.

Ranking factors: Listings with bright, clear, well-lit photos rank higher in Facebook's Marketplace
feed algorithm. Dark, blurry, or cluttered photos are associated with lower-quality listings and
rank accordingly. Facebook's visual quality assessment runs automatically on uploaded images.

Prohibited: Text overlays on photos (phone numbers, prices, URLs, watermarks) violate Facebook's
photo policies and will trigger listing removal. Photos with contact information embedded in the
image are removed quickly and can result in account warnings.

First photo: The hero photo is displayed in the buyer's Marketplace feed as the primary thumbnail.
It must immediately identify the item and look appealing in a compact feed card format.

## 1stDibs

1stDibs is a premium luxury marketplace where buyers expect gallery-quality presentation and where
trust is built through visual professionalism. Items on 1stDibs are priced significantly higher
than comparable items on general resale platforms, and buyer expectations are calibrated to that
price point.

Minimum photos: 3 photos required for listing approval. In practice, a 3-photo submission will
receive lower placement than a full 10-12 photo submission.

Quality standard: Gallery-quality photography is expected. This means professional studio lighting
or controlled natural light, consistent white or neutral backgrounds, no visible distractions,
no amateur composition errors (tilted horizon, partial frame, harsh shadows).

Editorial preference: Styled editorial photos — item in context with complementary objects,
tastefully arranged — are preferred for decorative arts, furniture, and fashion. The visual
language of 1stDibs is aspirational.

Authentication: Items on 1stDibs must be authenticated before listing. Photos of maker's marks,
signatures, provenance documents, and authentication certificates are part of the expected photo
set for high-value items.

## Chairish

Chairish specializes in vintage and antique furniture, decor, and art. Its buyer base is
design-conscious — interior designers, decorators, and serious collectors represent a significant
portion of the buyer pool.

Quality standard: Professional-quality photos are expected. Blurry, dark, or poorly composed
photos are actively penalized in Chairish's listing review and ranking systems.

Context preference: Styled room-setting photos are strongly preferred for furniture. Showing a
dining table set for a meal, a vintage lamp on a styled side table, or a rug in a curated living
room context outperforms the same item photographed in a garage or storage unit.

Multiple angles required: For furniture, Chairish expects front, side, back, detail, and condition
documentation photos at minimum. Listings missing critical angles are flagged during the review
process.

Condition documentation: Chairish requires honest condition documentation. Photos must show all
significant wear, damage, or repairs. Listings that misrepresent condition through selective
photography are removed and the seller's account is penalized.

## Instagram and Social Commerce

Instagram and social commerce platforms (Pinterest, TikTok Shop) operate on a visual attention
economy. The first half-second of visual impression determines whether a viewer engages.

Aspect ratios: Square 1:1 for grid posts (1080x1080px minimum), vertical 4:5 for feed posts that
maximize screen real estate (1080x1350px), vertical 9:16 for Stories and Reels (1080x1920px).

Lifestyle context is mandatory: Items photographed in aspirational lifestyle contexts outperform
item-only photos on social platforms by a significant margin. A vintage mid-century chair in a
beautifully lit living room setting will generate dramatically more engagement than the same chair
on a white background.

First impression rule: The visual hook must land in the first frame. On Instagram and TikTok, the
algorithm reads engagement signals in the first 1-3 seconds. A compelling, visually arresting hero
image or opening video frame is the difference between algorithmic amplification and suppression.

Hashtag-searchable captions: Social platforms use captions as metadata. Descriptive captions with
relevant search terms (style era, category, material, color) improve discoverability.

## What Gets Listings Removed Across Platforms

The following photo violations result in listing removal or account warnings across most major
resale platforms:

Stock photos used for used or one-of-a-kind items. Platforms detect and flag stock image matches.

Watermarks with competitor platform names or seller contact information. This is a policy violation
on eBay, Facebook, Etsy, and Chairish.

Contact information embedded in photos — phone numbers, email addresses, website URLs. This is a
near-universal violation that results in immediate removal.

Prohibited item imagery — photos that make a prohibited item identifiable, even if the listing
description attempts to obscure the category.

Misleading photos — photos of a different item than described, photos that hide significant damage,
photos cropped to conceal material defects. All major platforms treat this as policy fraud.

AI-generated photos presented as real item photos — this is an emerging policy area, but major
platforms are increasingly flagging and removing AI-generated product images used to misrepresent
actual item condition or appearance.
