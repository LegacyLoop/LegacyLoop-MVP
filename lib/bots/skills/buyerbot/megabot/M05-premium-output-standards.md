# BuyerBot MegaBot Skill: Premium Output Standards

## Purpose

A BuyerBot MegaBot output is a specialist buyer match report, not a list of
platforms to try. The difference between a generic recommendation and a
specialist report is specificity — specific buyer profiles, specific platforms
with active buyer pools, specific outreach language calibrated to each platform
and buyer type, and specific conversion tactics grounded in how buyers in each
category actually make purchase decisions. This skill defines the structure and
content standards every MegaBot BuyerBot output must meet.

## The Seven-Section Report Structure

Every MegaBot BuyerBot output contains seven sections in this order. Each
section has defined content requirements. Sections may not be omitted for
brevity. A report that omits a section is incomplete.

### Section 1: Item Classification and Market Position

Before buyer profiles are presented, the report establishes what the item is
and where it stands in the current market. This section is two to four
sentences. It is written for a seller who may not have deep category knowledge.

Content requirements: item category and sub-category (not a description of the
item itself — the seller already knows what they have), the current market
health for this category (active, thin, trending, seasonal), the item's
position within the category (common, mid-range, specialist, rare), and the
confidence level of the demand analysis.

Example of this section done correctly:

"This piece falls within the Arts and Crafts movement pottery category, a
specialist collector market with active auction coverage and sustained buyer
demand over the past decade. Items from this maker and period are not common
on the open market — fewer than twelve comparable pieces have sold through
documented channels in the past two years — which positions this item above
the mid-range of the category. Our demand analysis carries high confidence
based on consistent transaction evidence and active collector community
engagement."

### Section 2: Buyer Profiles

This section presents two to four buyer profiles. Each profile represents a
distinct buyer segment that MegaBot has identified for this item. Profiles are
ordered from highest to lowest estimated willingness-to-pay.

Each profile contains the following fields, presented consistently:

Segment name: A descriptive label that helps the seller understand who this
buyer is. Not a demographic label. A behavioral and motivational description.
Example: "Specialist period collector" or "Interior designer sourcing for
client project" or "Institutional acquirer with active acquisition mandate."

Platform presence: Where this buyer type is most likely to be found and
active. Specific, not generic. Not "on social media" but "active in the
r/potteryidentification and r/artsandcrafts subreddits, and in the Arts and
Crafts Society member forum."

Intent level: Rated on a three-point scale. Purchase-ready (has expressed
active interest in acquiring), evaluating (aware of the category, comparing
options), or aspirational (desires ownership but not currently in active
search mode).

Estimated willingness-to-pay: A dollar range based on triangulated signal
evidence. This is not a guess. It references the signals that support it.
Example: "Estimated at $3,200 to $4,800 based on five comparable sales at
established auction and three documented private sale transactions."

Decision timeline: How quickly this buyer type typically moves from first
contact to transaction close. Varies significantly by segment.

Relationship to item: What this buyer values about the specific item —
investment quality, display value, completion of a collection, professional
use, or personal connection to the category.

### Section 3: Hot Leads

Hot leads are specific, actionable buyer opportunities — not segments but
individual channels or communities where MegaBot has identified concentrated
buyer activity right now. This section contains three to six hot leads.

Each hot lead specifies:
- The platform and specific community, subreddit, group, or channel
- The current activity level (number of recent WTB posts, active discussion
  threads, or community size with engagement rate)
- The recommended action (post a listing, initiate a direct message, submit
  to the community's weekly sales thread, or contact the community moderator
  about a featured listing opportunity)
- The expected response rate based on category norms for this community

Hot leads are time-sensitive. They reflect market conditions at the time of
the scan. MegaBot notes the scan date prominently in this section and
recommends acting within seven days to capture the current activity window.

### Section 4: Platform Opportunities

This section ranks the platforms with the most active buyer pool for this
specific item, with justification for each ranking. It is not a generic list
of where to list items. It is a specific analysis of where buyers for this
item are concentrated right now.

For each recommended platform, the report includes:

Active buyer pool assessment: How many qualified buyers for this specific item
category are currently active on this platform, based on recent search volume,
transaction volume, and community activity.

Price ceiling on this platform: What buyers on this platform are willing to
pay, based on comparable sales. Some platforms consistently clear at lower
prices than others. Sellers deserve to know this before choosing where to invest
listing time.

Transaction velocity: How quickly items in this category and price range
typically transact on this platform. Days-to-sale estimate based on recent
comparable data.

Listing optimization notes: What format, title structure, or listing elements
perform best for this item type on this platform. Not generic SEO advice.
Platform-specific and category-specific guidance.

Platform-specific risks: What commonly goes wrong when listing this item type
on this platform. Underpricing due to buyer pressure, platform policy issues,
authentication requirements, or buyer demographic mismatch.

### Section 5: Outreach Strategies

This section provides per-platform messaging frameworks calibrated to each
buyer type. It does not provide fill-in-the-blank templates — it provides the
strategic intent, tone, and key elements of effective first contact for each
platform and buyer segment combination.

The critical principle governing this section: the message that opens a
conversation is not a sales pitch. It is an introduction that signals
credibility. A serious buyer receiving first contact from a seller they do not
know will evaluate the message for evidence that the seller knows what they
have. A message that demonstrates category knowledge ("I recognize this as a
period piece from the Rookwood pottery Cincinnati operation, with the flame
mark indicating the 1905 to 1910 production window") immediately differentiates
the seller from casual listers.

For each platform and buyer segment, this section specifies:

Opening approach: How to initiate contact in a way that is appropriate for
the platform's communication norms and the buyer's sophistication level.

Content to lead with: The specific aspect of the item to feature first, based
on what this buyer segment values most. Not a full description — the one fact
that is most likely to earn a response.

What to avoid: The specific mistakes that cause serious buyers to dismiss
outreach from this type of seller. These are typically generic statements
("great condition, make an offer"), pricing errors (listing without a price
in channels where that signals inexperience), or missing documentation
references.

Documentation to offer: What supporting material to proactively offer in
first contact. Appraisal documentation, provenance letters, original receipts,
family history letters, and similar materials should be offered proactively
with serious buyers — they expect it and its absence raises concerns.

### Section 6: Response Rate Optimization

This section addresses the gap between sending outreach and receiving qualified
responses. Outreach volume does not produce results — outreach quality and
timing do.

Timing guidance: When in the week and day do buyers in this category engage
with messages. This varies by platform and by buyer type. Specialist collectors
often engage on weekend mornings when they have time to evaluate. Dealer buyers
often prefer Tuesday through Thursday communication during business hours.

Follow-up protocol: When to follow up after no response, how many times, and
at what intervals. Different buyer segments have different norms. Over-following
up with a specialist collector damages the relationship. Under-following up with
a dealer buyer who processes high volume loses the inquiry.

Subject line and opening line performance: For email outreach, what categories
of subject lines produce open rates above thirty percent for this type of item.
The seller's name and item category in the subject line consistently outperforms
generic lines. A reference to a recent sale ("Following recent comparable sales
at [auction house]") signals market awareness.

Friction reduction: What one or two things a seller can do to make it easier
for a serious buyer to say yes quickly. For high-value items: offer a condition
report by video call before purchase. For shipping-sensitive items: provide
three carrier quotes with insurance options. Remove any uncertainty that a
serious buyer would need to resolve before committing.

### Section 7: Closing and Conversion Tactics

A buyer who has expressed interest is not a closed transaction. This section
covers the specific tactics that move interested buyers to commitment.

Price anchoring: For items where negotiation is expected, how to set the
initial ask and how to respond to low offers without damaging the relationship
or the buyer's interest. The goal is not to win the negotiation — it is to
close the transaction at a price the seller is satisfied with. A seller who
wins the negotiation but loses the buyer has achieved nothing.

Creating appropriate urgency: Urgency tactics that are credible and specific
outperform generic pressure. "I have a second inquiry arriving from a collector
in Boston who expressed interest at this price" is a credible urgency signal
if true. "Act now before it's gone" is not. MegaBot only authorizes urgency
framing that is grounded in factual market conditions — a genuine competing
inquiry, a documented comparable sale that has since moved market conditions,
or a specific listing window after which the channel strategy will change.

Managing multiple serious inquiries: When more than one qualified buyer
expresses interest simultaneously, how to manage the process fairly and
legally, and how to communicate the situation to all parties without creating
a pressure dynamic that causes buyers to withdraw.

## Before and After: Amateur vs. Specialist Language

The difference in seller credibility between amateur and specialist language
is substantial. These examples illustrate the contrast.

Category: Vintage camera. Item: Leica M3 Double Stroke, 1954, excellent
mechanical condition, some brassing on body.

Amateur approach:
"Vintage Leica camera for sale. Works great. Very old and rare. Make an offer.
Shipping available. Local pickup preferred. No low-ballers."

What a specialist buyer reads in this message: the seller does not know the
specific variant (double stroke vs. single stroke is meaningful to this buyer),
does not know the production year (collectors date these precisely), uses
vague condition language ("works great" tells a serious buyer nothing), and
signals price anxiety with "no low-ballers," which suggests the seller is
uncertain about value and vulnerable to negotiation pressure. The specialist
buyer either moves on or makes a low offer knowing the seller is underinformed.

Specialist approach:
"1954 Leica M3 double stroke, serial number range confirming first-production-
year manufacture. Mechanically excellent — all speeds accurate, rangefinder
bright and accurate, curtains intact with no pinholes. Body shows honest use
brassing at advance lever and strap lugs consistent with age and field use.
Clean glass throughout, no haze or separation. Sourced from the estate of a
professional photographer active in New England from the 1950s through 1980s.
Documentation available."

What a specialist buyer reads in this message: a seller who understands what
they have, has done the documentation work, and will be straightforward to
transact with. The buyer's risk perception drops. Their willingness to pay
rises. The transaction is likely to close at or near asking price because the
seller has removed the uncertainty that justifies a discount.

Every MegaBot BuyerBot output includes category-specific language guidance that
helps the seller communicate at the specialist level for their specific item.
This guidance is grounded in the vocabulary and information priorities of the
actual buyer community for that item — not generic advice, but the specific
things that buyers in this market look for and expect to see.

The standard BuyerBot output is the document a seller shows to a sophisticated
estate attorney, an auction house specialist, or a dealer partner as evidence
that the item has been properly researched and is ready for market. It should
be specific enough to be taken seriously by those professionals, and clear
enough that a seller without domain expertise can execute on its recommendations.
