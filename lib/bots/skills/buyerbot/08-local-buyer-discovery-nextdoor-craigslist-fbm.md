# Local Buyers Are the Right Answer for Heavy/Bulky/Fragile Items

A 200-pound credenza isn't worth shipping. A 9-foot dining table can't be shipped. Fragile pottery would cost $80 to ship for a $40 sale. For these, right buyer is local — within 1-2 hours.

## The Three Hyper-Local Sources

### 1. Facebook Marketplace + Local FB Groups
Largest hyper-local source. See skill 03.

### 2. Craigslist
Older, less popular, still has serious local buyers:
- Tradespeople buying tools
- Estate hunters buying antiques
- Restorers buying projects
- Cash-only preferences

**How to find buyers:**
- Browse "wanted" (craigslist.org/[city]/wan)
- Search "WTB [item]" within wanted
- Browse "for sale by owner" to see local pricing baselines

**Response patterns:** email relay, most active 6-9pm local, faster in cities, lower buyer quality (more scammers).

### 3. Nextdoor
Underrated hyper-local source. Neighborhood-level network.

**How to find buyers:**
- Search Marketplace for "WTB [item]"
- Browse "For Sale & Free" section
- Post directly — Nextdoor users respond quickly (personal connections)

**Advantages:** Real names + verified addresses, hyper-local pickup (5-10 min), higher trust than Craigslist. Strong for furniture, appliances, sports equipment, tools.

## Cross-Platform Local Search

For heavy/bulky items, run in parallel:
1. Facebook Marketplace
2. Facebook local groups
3. Craigslist Wanted + For Sale
4. Nextdoor Marketplace + neighborhood posts
5. Local subreddit (r/[city])
6. Local "Buy Nothing" or "Free" groups

## Identifying Local Active Buyers

Beyond WTB posts, look for:
- Repeated category posts over 3-6 months
- Comments on local for-sale posts
- Active in local hobby clubs (woodworking, antique, car)
- Local estate sale attendees
- Local antique mall booth owners

## Local Pickup Logistics

When recommending local buyers:
- Daylight only (security)
- Public location for small items (coffee shop, mall parking)
- Home pickup for large items (with someone present)
- Cash or PayPal Goods & Services
- Photo ID exchange for high-value
- Help loading (buyer brings help)

## Local Hauler Networks

For items too big for seller to deliver:
- **GoShare** — peer-to-peer delivery
- **TaskRabbit** — local delivery taskers
- **Lugg** — furniture-specific moving
- **Roadie** — gig delivery

Matches local-pickup-only items to buyers 100-200 miles away willing to pay delivery.

## Output

Each local lead: platform (specific source), identifier (name + distance from seller), intent_signal (quote their post), intent_recency_days, contact_path (platform message link), match_score, match_reason.

Aim for 2-4 local leads when item is bulky/heavy/local-pickup-friendly. Skip when national shipping wins.
