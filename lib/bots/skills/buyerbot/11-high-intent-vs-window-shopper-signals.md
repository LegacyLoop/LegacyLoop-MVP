# Not Every Real Person Is a Real Buyer

You can find a real human with a real handle and real intent — and they STILL might be a window shopper who never buys anything. Filter for buyers likely to convert.

## High-Intent Signals

**1. Explicit budget statement**
"Willing to pay up to $2,000" / "Budget around $1,500"
Named number = real. Window shoppers don't name numbers.

**2. Specific item identification**
"Looking for a 1958-1962 CH-25 in original paper cord, not later production"
Specificity = research = serious.

**3. Logistics planning**
"Can drive up to 6 hours from Boston" / "Will pay shipping"
Pre-thinking logistics = mentally committed.

**4. Recent purchase history**
Profile shows they've BOUGHT things past 6 months. Past behavior predicts future.

**5. Questions about authentication**
"How do you confirm it's not a reproduction?"
Serious collector mentality.

**6. Time-sensitive context**
"Need it by end of month for my project"
Timeline = commitment.

**7. Multiple platform presence**
Same person active on r/MCMFurniture AND Facebook MCM group AND Instagram = serious enthusiast.

**8. Forum tenure**
1,000+ posts since 2015 = veteran buyer.

## Window Shopper Signals

**1. Vague interest**
"I love things like this" / "These are so cool"
No specifics. Skip.

**2. "Saving up" language**
"Hopefully one day I can afford" / "Adding to dream list"
Not buying now. Skip.

**3. Comments without questions**
"Beautiful!" / "Wow love it"
Appreciation, not intent. Skip.

**4. Tire-kicking history**
Years of "wow!" comments but ZERO purchases. Watches but doesn't buy.

**5. Low engagement**
3 posts in 2 years. Not actively shopping.

**6. Lowball history**
"$50 for that" on $500 items. Unrealistic. Skip unless dealer pricing fits.

**7. "I'll think about it" pattern**
Asks price, ghosts. Time-waster.

## The Speed Test

Active buyers respond within hours. Window shoppers take days or never. Check their response patterns on past posts.

## The Commitment Test

Seller should ask qualifying question early ("Does your space have room for 60-inch credenza?"). Real buyers answer. Window shoppers ghost.

## The 80/20 Rule

80% of "interested-looking people" are window shoppers. Only 20% buy. Your job: surface the 20%.

25 candidates → 5 pass high-intent filter → ship 5 leads. Better than 25 mixed.

## matchScore Modifiers

High-intent signals (add):
- Explicit budget: +10
- Specific item ID: +8
- Logistics pre-planning: +6
- Recent purchase history: +5
- Authentication questions: +4
- Time-sensitive context: +3
- Cross-platform consistency: +5
- Forum tenure: +5

Window shopper signals (subtract):
- Vague interest: -15
- "Saving up" language: -20
- Comments without questions: -10
- Tire-kicking history: -25
- Lowball history: -15

Baseline 70. Final range 70-100 normal. Below 60 = filter out.

## Don't Pad

3 high-intent leads → ship 3. Don't backfill with window shoppers.

"Found 3 high-intent leads and 8 window-shoppers (filtered). Pursue the 3 first." > "Found 11 leads, scores range 90 to 45..."

Curated > noisy.
