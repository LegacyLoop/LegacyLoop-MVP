# Instagram Buyers Are Visual, Aspirational, and Often Wealthy

Instagram is where decorators, designers, stylists, and aesthetic-driven collectors live. They're not posting WTB threads — they're following accounts, hashtagging finds, and DMing sellers privately.

## Hashtag Discovery

**Primary hashtags:**
- Vintage furniture: #vintagefurniture #midcenturymodern #mcmfurniture #danishmodern
- Vintage decor: #vintagedecor #vintagehome #brownfurniture
- Vintage clothing: #vintageclothing #vintagestyle #vintagefashion
- Jewelry: #vintagejewelry #estatejewelry #signedjewelry
- Art: #vintageart #originalart #midcenturyart
- Cameras: #filmcamera #vintagecamera #believeinfilm

**Specific hashtags (narrower, higher-intent):**
#hanswegner, #ch25, #teakfurniture, #stickleyfurniture, #leicam3

**Local hashtags:** #vintageboston, #vintagemaine, #vintageNYC, #portlandvintage

## Step 1: Find Active Hashtag Users

View recent posts (past 14 days) for each hashtag. For each:
- Note poster handle
- Classify: SELLER (shop link in bio, "DM for price") or BUYER/COLLECTOR (personal photos, "thrift haul!")

Pull buyer/collector handles.

## Step 2: Comment-Section Mining

When a popular vintage seller posts an item, comments are full of buyers:
- "How much?" → price-curious
- "Is this still available?" → buying-mode
- "I've been looking for this!" → hot lead
- "Wish I had room" → warm lead for similar items

Pull commenter handles for similar-item outreach.

Works especially for mid-century furniture, vintage cameras, watches, jewelry.

## Step 3: Profile Vetting

Once you have a handle, visit profile:
- Bio mentions of hobby
- Recent posts showing personal collection
- Following list (similar accounts?)
- Story highlights (collection tours)
- Location

3+ category posts past 90 days + follows 5+ similar accounts + reasonable region = real lead.

## Step 4: Engagement-Based Discovery

Big accounts have buyer pools larger than seller audiences:
- @1stdibs (furniture)
- @chairish_official
- @vintagestyleeveryday
- @hodinkee (watches)
- @analogfilm (cameras)

Mine THEIR comment sections for active buyers in your category.

## Step 5: Instagram DM Etiquette

- More personal — expect quality, not volume
- Image-friendly — lead with photo
- Story-replies as opener (more personal than cold DM)
- Brief and visual — long text feels like spam

**Sample DM:**
Hey! Saw your comment on @midcenturyloves about loving teak credenzas — I have one (photo attached) for sale. 1965 Hundevad, original condition, asking $1,800. Let me know if you'd like more pics or want to chat.

## Pinterest Bonus Signal

Pinterest users PIN what they want. Can't DM reliably, but search your category to see active boards — high pin count = visual demand. Mention in scan summary as ambient signal.

## Output

Each Instagram lead populates the standard schema: platform "Instagram", identifier (@handle), intent_signal (comment quote or profile pattern), intent_recency_days, contact_path (IG DM link), match_score, match_reason.

Aim for 2-5 Instagram leads for visually-driven categories. Skip for tools/books/electronics.
