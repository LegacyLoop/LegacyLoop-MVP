# Garage Sale Buyer Matching

## Three Buyer Outreach Strategies

BuyerBot must match outreach strategy to the selling context:

### Online Selling — National/International Reach
Standard BuyerBot behavior — find buyers across all connected platforms, marketplace listings, collector forums, and our network. Cast the widest net. This is the existing behavior and remains unchanged.

### Garage Sale — Local Buyers Only
When the seller is preparing for a garage sale, yard sale, or estate sale:

**Primary channels:**
- Facebook local buy/sell/trade groups (within 25 miles)
- Nextdoor neighborhood posts
- Local Craigslist community section
- Neighborhood flyer distribution (digital + print-ready)
- Word of mouth — "tell your neighbors"

**Post format for local channels:**
"GARAGE SALE — [Date] [Time] [Address]
[3-5 highlight items with prices]
Cash preferred. Everything must go.
Come early for best selection!"

**For high-value items at a local sale:**
Create individual local marketplace posts BEFORE the sale date. A $200 piece of furniture deserves its own Facebook Marketplace post with photos, not just a line on the garage sale flyer.

### Quick Sale — Immediate Local Pickup
When the seller wants items gone TODAY:

**Framing:** "Items available for immediate local pickup"
**Channels:** Facebook Marketplace (mark as available NOW), OfferUp (set to local pickup only), Craigslist (free section for true giveaways)
**Key phrase:** "First come, first served. Pickup today."

## The Antique/Collectible Exception

Items flagged by AntiqueBot or CollectiblesBot should ALWAYS get the online selling treatment, even if the seller is planning a garage sale. Collectors are national buyers. A Tiffany lamp in Waterville, Maine will sell for its full value to a buyer in New York who finds it online. Do not limit these items to local outreach.
