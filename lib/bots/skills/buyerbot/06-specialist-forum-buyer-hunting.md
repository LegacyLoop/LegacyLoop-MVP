# Forums Are Where the Real Money Lives

For high-value collectibles, the most serious buyers don't hang out on Reddit, Facebook, or Instagram. They're on dedicated specialist forums. The top buyers in any specialty are forum regulars. Highest-conversion leads live here.

## The Forum Map by Category

**Audio/Hi-Fi:** AudioKarma, AudioAsylum, diyAudio, Steve Hoffman Music Forums, Audiogon

**Watches:** WatchUSeek (largest), TimeZone, Watchnet, TheWatchSite

**Cameras:** Photo.net, Rangefinder Forum (Leica), LFPF (Large Format), Fred Miranda Forums

**Tools (Hand/Woodworking):** WoodCentral, SawmillCreek, WoodNet, OldTools mailing list, owwm.org

**Vintage Furniture:** Apartment Therapy forums, Fine Furniture Maker forums

**Cars:** Make-specific forums (mustangforums, miata.net, jeep-cj), Bring a Trailer, Hemmings

**Vinyl:** Steve Hoffman, Discogs forums, VinylCollective

**Knives:** BladeForums, USN (Usual Suspects Network), KnifeHogs

**Pens:** Fountain Pen Network (FPN), Pen Addict

**Action Figures/Toys:** Action Figure Authority (AFA), GIJoeForum, TheRPF

**Coins:** CoinTalk, CollectorsUniverse, US Coin Forum

## How Forums Work

Most specialist forums have:
1. General discussion subforums
2. WTB/WTS subforums (literal wanted-to-buy)
3. Identification/Help subforums (researchers → future buyers)
4. Newbie introduction subforums (eager new buyers)
5. Show off your collection subforums (active collectors)

Priority: WTB → Identification → Newbie → Show off → General.

## Step 1: Find the WTB Subforum

Browse past 30 days. Each post is a hot lead. Pull:
- Username, post URL, post date
- Exact item they're looking for
- Stated budget
- Location
- Forum PM link

## Step 2: Mine Identification Threads

"Found this at an estate sale — what is it worth?" → researching to buy → warm lead
"Picked this up for $XX, did I do okay?" → ACTIVE buyer → hot lead

## Step 3: Active Member Profile Mining

Filter for:
- Post count > 100 (committed)
- Join date 2+ years ago
- Recent posts past 30 days (still active)
- Posts heavily about your category

Veteran collectors. High-conversion outreach.

## Step 4: Forum PM Etiquette

1. Be a forum member if possible (drive-by registrations distrusted)
2. Reference forum context ("saw your WTB thread for Type 11 Stanley #4")
3. Full sentences, proper grammar
4. Detailed photos
5. Mention provenance and history
6. Patient — 1-3 days response time is normal

## Step 5: Forum Marketplace Listings

Many forums have for-sale subforums. Fair-market pricing, pre-vetted enthusiast buyers. Recommend as listing option alongside direct outreach.

## Output

Each forum lead populates the standard schema: platform (forum name + category), identifier (forum username + post count), intent_signal (WTB thread quote), intent_recency_days, contact_path (forum PM URL), match_score, match_reason.

Aim for 2-4 forum leads per scan when active forums exist. Often highest-converting in entire scan.
