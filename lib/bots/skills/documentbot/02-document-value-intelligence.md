# How Documents Affect Item Value

## Value Multipliers by Document Type

### Original Receipt Present
- **Online:** Provides retail anchor context — buyers can see original price
- **Garage sale:** "Paid $X new" increases buyer trust and justifies asking price
- **Value impact:** +context rather than direct price increase, but reduces negotiation pressure

### Certificate of Authenticity Present
- **Collectibles:** Essential for commanding price premiums. Without it, value drops 30-50%
- **Antiques:** Validates provenance claims and period attribution
- **Value impact:** +10-30% for certified items vs uncertified equivalent

### Original Manual Present
- **Electronics:** +5-10% online value (buyer confidence in setup and troubleshooting)
- **Appliances:** +5-15% (reassurance of ongoing support and proper use)
- **Collectibles:** Significant for completeness collectors — original box + manual can double value
- **Value impact:** Moderate but consistent trust signal

### Professional Appraisal Present
- **Sets a price floor** — never recommend below a professional appraisal value
- **Use for White Glove services** as the pricing basis
- **Insurance documentation** — valuable for high-end items
- **Value impact:** Validates asking price, prevents lowball offers

### Maintenance Records Present (vehicles and appliances)
- **Vehicles:** Major trust signal, documented service history can add 5-15% to sale price
- **Appliances:** "Well maintained" confirmation supports higher asking price
- **Value impact:** Condition grade may increase one full level with documented maintenance

### Provenance Documents Present
- **Antiques:** Can multiply value dramatically — a documented ownership chain from a notable collection transforms pricing
- **Estate items:** Chain of custody establishes legal selling authority
- **Art:** Critical for authentication — without provenance, attribution is uncertain
- **Value impact:** Can be the difference between $500 and $5,000 for the right item
