# Premium Output Standards: What Serious Collectors Expect

## Purpose

A MegaBot collectibles output will be read by people who know their category deeply. Hobbyists, dealers, collectors, and investors in trading cards, coins, comics, watches, and similar categories have precise language expectations. Imprecise or generic language destroys credibility instantly. This skill defines the structural requirements, required sections, and terminology standards for MegaBot collectibles outputs that meet professional expectations.

---

## The Professional PSA Submission Analysis: Reference Standard

A PSA submission analysis, as produced by a professional dealer or grader evaluating whether to submit an item, has a consistent structure. MegaBot collectibles output should meet or exceed this standard.

A professional PSA submission analysis covers:

1. Item identification: set name, year, card number, player/subject, variation designation if applicable.
2. Centering assessment: front and back centering expressed as approximate ratios (e.g., 58/42 left-right, 54/46 top-bottom). Notation of whether centering appears to meet PSA 10 threshold (60/40 front, 65/35 back for most post-1980 issues).
3. Corner assessment: each of the four corners evaluated individually. Notation of any fraying, creasing, soft rounding, or wear. Expressed as "four sharp corners" or with specific notation of which corner shows wear.
4. Edge assessment: all four edges evaluated. Notation of any nicks, chips, roughness, or factory defects. Expressed as "clean edges throughout" or with specific notation of the defect location.
5. Surface assessment: both front and back. Notation of any scratches, print lines, stains, creases, foil damage (for chrome/prizm), or cloudiness. Photo limitations acknowledged where surface cannot be definitively assessed.
6. Grade estimate: PSA equivalent grade with confidence percentage. Half-point estimates expressed as the range spanning the uncertainty (e.g., "likely PSA 8 to 9, modal 8.5 equivalent under BGS standards").
7. Submission recommendation: yes or no, with the financial case (break-even grade, upside scenario, downside scenario, current PSA service pricing).

## The Heritage Auctions Catalog Entry: Reference Standard

Heritage produces catalog descriptions for significant lots that function as expert opinions in written form. These entries are the gold standard for collectibles description language. A MegaBot output for a high-value item should match the precision and completeness of a Heritage catalog description.

A Heritage catalog entry for a key card includes:

- Headline: player name, year, set name, card number, and grade. "1952 Topps Mickey Mantle #311 PSA NM-MT 8."
- Visual condition narrative: a precise description of the card's appearance, starting with the most positive characteristics and noting condition issues with specific location references. "Four sharp corners. Centering is strong at approximately 55/45 left to right on the face and 60/40 top to bottom. Surface exhibits the expected gloss of an unplayed example with no visible scratches under standard examination. A faint print defect is present in the upper right quadrant of the reverse."
- Historical context: where the card fits in the collector's landscape. The significance of the set, the player, and the grade. For key cards, the provenance narrative.
- Market context: what comparable examples have realized at auction, when, and through which platform. Expressed with specifics, not generalities.
- Population context: where this grade sits in the known supply. "With a PSA 8 population of 47 and only 12 graded higher across all services, this example represents a realistic point of entry into a set that has seen strong appreciation."

## Required Sections for MegaBot Collectibles Output

Every MegaBot collectibles output must contain the following sections, in this order:

### Section 1: Item Identification

Category, set/series name, year of issue, item number or designation, player/subject/title, specific variation or parallel designation if applicable. Do not use "this card" or "this item" without first establishing the full identification. Be specific.

Example (correct): "2018-19 Panini Prizm #280 Luka Doncic Rookie Card, Silver Prizm parallel."
Example (incorrect): "Luka Doncic rookie card."

### Section 2: Visual Grading Sub-Scores

For cards: centering (front and back), corners (each corner), edges (all four), surface (front and back). Each sub-category receives a rating on a 1-10 sub-scale with a brief explanatory note.

For comics: cover gloss, staple integrity, spine condition, corner wear, page color (white/off-white/cream/brittle), structural issues.

For coins: strike quality, surface preservation (luster for mint state, reflectivity for proofs), contact marks (major and minor), eye appeal. Each on the Sheldon sub-scale.

For watches: case condition (polished/unpolished, scratches, dings), dial condition (fading, tritium plots, indices, text clarity), crystal condition, bracelet completeness and condition, movement condition (if documentation available).

### Section 3: Grade Estimate with Confidence

The consensus grade estimate from four-agent analysis, expressed as a specific grade or grade range with a confidence percentage. The confidence percentage must reflect the degree of agent agreement and the limitations of photographic assessment.

Structure: "Consensus grade estimate: PSA 9 (modal), range PSA 8.5-9. Confidence: 74 percent. Confidence is limited by the chrome surface, which requires physical inspection for definitive surface assessment."

### Section 4: Graded Values Table

A table or structured list showing estimated realized value at each plausible grade level. Must cover at minimum the three most likely grades (modal estimate, one grade above, one grade below).

Structure:
- PSA 10: $[value range], based on [N] recent APR comps, most recent: $[price] on [approximate date]
- PSA 9: $[value range], based on [N] comps
- PSA 8: $[value range], based on [N] comps
- If BGS is more applicable: BGS 10 Black Label, BGS 9.5, BGS 9 values listed separately

### Section 5: Raw Values

The estimated value of the item in its current ungraded condition. Must be expressed separately from graded values. Cite source (PriceCharting median, eBay sold comps, Beckett). Include the raw-to-graded gap for the modal grade estimate.

### Section 6: Grading ROI Math

Submission cost at current PSA pricing for the appropriate service tier. Expected turnaround at time of analysis. Break-even grade. Net upside at modal grade after fees. Net outcome if grades one grade below modal. Recommendation: submit or sell raw.

This section must always show the math. A recommendation without the calculation is not acceptable.

### Section 7: Platform Routing

Where should this item be sold, and in what format? The recommendation must be specific to the item's characteristics:

- High-value graded item (above $500): Heritage, PWCC, or Goldin auction recommended. eBay acceptable but auction house likely to achieve better realized price.
- Mid-range graded item ($100-500): eBay Buy It Now or 7-day auction. Reference current listings volume.
- Raw item below $100: eBay, PriceCharting buyer pool, or local card show.
- Category-specific routing: Chrono24 for watches, Discogs for vinyl, GoCollect marketplace for comics.

### Section 8: Pop Context

The current PSA (or applicable service) population data for the item at the estimated grade and above. Commentary on what the population means for pricing and liquidity. If population is disputed or unavailable, state this explicitly.

### Section 9: Investment Outlook

A directional assessment of whether the item is appreciating, stable, or depreciating based on market trend data. Not a financial recommendation. A factual statement about observed market direction. Should cite the basis (6-month price trend from available comp data, pop report trajectory, player/subject market conditions, set market conditions).

Appropriate: "Market trend for this player's key rookies is slightly upward over the past 12 months, with PSA 10 examples up approximately 15 percent from the 12-month low. No major catalysts (career milestone, Hall of Fame eligibility) are imminent that would suggest an acceleration."

Inappropriate: "This card should go up in value." "Good investment."

### Section 10: Authentication Recommendation

For signed items: whether authentication is recommended and which service is appropriate. For unsigned items: whether any aspect of the item (manufacturing characteristics, surface appearance, variation designation) warrants verification before sale. For coins: PCGS or NGC verification if uncertified. For comics: CGC submission recommendation with census context.

## Language That Serious Collectors Trust: Category Terminology

### Trading Cards

Use: centering ratio, surface, corners, edges, print line, foil scratch, centering off, short print, SP, SSP, population, pop report, APR, comp, realized, parallel, insert, base, refractor, prizm, chrome, auto (autograph), RC (rookie card), holo, crease, pinhole.

Avoid: pretty, nice, clean (without qualification), valuable, rare (without documentation), looks like a 10.

### Comics

Use: CGC, CBCS, census, universal slab, restored label, SS (signature series), key issue, first appearance, origin issue, condition code (VF, NM, MT), staple rust, spine stress, color touch, page quality, brittleness, centerfold detached, toning.

Avoid: in good shape, well-preserved (without grade context), very old.

### Coins

Use: Sheldon scale, MS (Mint State), PR or PF (Proof), strike, luster, contact marks, bag marks, eye appeal, cameo, deep cameo (DCAM), toning (natural vs. artificial), details grade, cleaned, Full Steps, Full Bands, Full Head, date, mint mark, die variety.

Avoid: shiny, looks uncirculated, silver coin (without denomination and date context).

### Watches

Use: reference number, caliber, movement, bracelet vs. strap, signed crown, tritium plots, luminova, patina, tropical dial, service history, box and papers (B&P), original dial, chapter ring, case back, pushers, bezel insert, water resistance rating.

Avoid: luxury watch, nice condition, working order (without service documentation reference).

### Sneakers

Use: DS (deadstock), VNDS (very near deadstock), OG (original colorway), crease, toe box, sole yellowing, glue separation, insole condition, lace replacement, original box, lid damaged, J-box, shape, toebox.

Avoid: barely worn, like new, great condition (without specific condition code).

## Before and After: Amateur vs. Specialist Grading Language

### Cards — Before (Amateur)

"This is a really nice looking Luka Doncic rookie card. The corners look good and the centering seems pretty even. It could be a PSA 10. Worth getting graded."

### Cards — After (Specialist)

"2018-19 Panini Prizm #280 Luka Doncic Silver Prizm RC. Centering photographs at approximately 57/43 left-right on the face, consistent with PSA 10 threshold, and approximately 56/44 top-bottom, within standard tolerance. Corners appear sharp at all four positions. Edges are clean throughout with no visible nicking. Surface assessment is inconclusive from available photography — chrome surfaces require direct-light physical inspection to rule out holo scratching. Consensus grade estimate: PSA 9 to 10, modal PSA 9.5 equivalent under BGS standards. PSA submission recommended at Collector service tier. Break-even requires PSA 9. Upside at PSA 10: net $[X] after fees. Current PSA 10 pop: 312."

### Coins — Before (Amateur)

"Old silver dollar. Looks uncirculated or close to it. Really shiny. Could be valuable."

### Coins — After (Specialist)

"1881-S Morgan Dollar. Obverse and reverse exhibit strong cartwheel luster consistent with an MS-65 or higher designation. Strike appears full with strong definition on the eagle feathers and Liberty hair detail. No visible contact marks in the fields under standard examination, though coin photography compresses surface detail — controlled lighting review is recommended for grade assessment above MS-65. Eye appeal is strong. PCGS and NGC both maintain populations in this date/mint mark that support active secondary market pricing. Current PCGS MS-65 population: 4,812. MS-66: 892. MS-67: 41. Estimated value: MS-64 $[X], MS-65 $[X], MS-66 $[X]. Grading submission recommended if current holder is raw."

## Output Tone and Precision Standards

Collector-grade outputs are factual, specific, and precise. They do not use superlatives. They do not express enthusiasm. They do not speculate without labeling speculation as such. They cite sources. They acknowledge limitations. They make recommendations with supporting data.

The MegaBot collectibles output is a professional analysis document. It should read like something a respected dealer or auction specialist wrote, not like a product listing or a social media post. Every claim should be traceable to a data source, a visual observation, or an explicit inference with stated assumptions.

When in doubt, be more specific, not less. Vagueness in collectibles analysis is read as ignorance by experienced buyers.
